This page highlights aromatic herbs and sculptural succulents. These five plants add fragrance, beauty, and resilience to your garden or windowsill. They're low-maintenance, unique, and each has its own character. Here's what they need to thrive.

French Lavender

  1. Sunlight: Full sun, 6-8 hrs
  2. Soil: Sandy, well-draining
  3. Water: Let dry out between waterings
  4. Fertilizer: Rarely needed
  5. Pruning: Light trim after bloom
  6. Cold: Tender perennial in 6a
  7. Pests: Root rot if overwatered

Mojito Mint

  1. Sunlight: Full to part sun
  2. Soil: Moist, rich soil
  3. Water: Keep evenly moist
  4. Fertilizer: Monthly light feed
  5. Trim regularly to maintain shape—and to have fresh sprigs ready for your next mojito.
  6. Cold: Hardy perennial
  7. Pests: Aphids, whiteflies

String of Pearls

  1. Sunlight: Bright indirect
  2. Soil: Cactus/succulent mix
  3. Water: Low; let dry fully
  4. Fertilizer: Once/month in spring/summer
  5. Pruning: Trim vines as needed
  6. Cold: Bring indoors for winter
  7. Pests: Mealybugs if overwatered

Gollum Jade

  1. Sunlight: Full sun
  2. Soil: Sandy, cactus soil
  3. Water: Very low needs
  4. Fertilizer: Monthly in summer
  5. Pruning: Pinch tips to shape
  6. Cold: Not frost-hardy
  7. Pests: Rare; watch for rot

Fairy Castle Cactus

  1. Sunlight: Full sun
  2. Soil: Fast-draining, dry soil
  3. Water: Once every 2–3 weeks
  4. Fertilizer: Only during active growth
  5. Pruning: Rarely needed
  6. Cold: Not cold tolerant
  7. Pests: Watch for scale, rot
