Herbs and succulents are rewarding, low-maintenance choices that thrive in containers and sunny windows. Whether fragrant or sculptural, these plants bring beauty and practicality to small spaces.

French Lavender

  1. Full sun (6-8 hours)
  2. Well-draining, sandy soil
  3. Low water; let dry between waterings
  4. Rarely needs fertilizer
  5. Trim lightly after blooming
  6. Overwinters in Zone 6a with protection
  7. Fragrant and drought-tolerant

Mojito Mint

  1. Full to part sun
  2. Moist, rich soil
  3. High water needs, especially in heat
  4. Fertilize occasionally
  5. Trim often to prevent flowering
  6. Spreads rapidly; great in containers. Even better in a Mojito.
  7. Perennial in Zone 6a

String of Pearls

  1. Bright, indirect light
  2. Succulent/cactus mix soil
  3. Low water; let soil dry completely
  4. No fertilizer needed unless leggy
  5. Trim stems to encourage fullness
  6. Bring indoors before temps drop below 50°F
  7. Beautiful in hanging containers

Gollum Jade

  1. Full sun to part shade
  2. Cactus or succulent soil
  3. Low water; drought-tolerant
  4. Fertilize sparingly
  5. Pinch to keep shape
  6. Bring inside before 40°F
  7. Unusual, sculptural foliage

Fairy Castle Cactus

  1. Full sun
  2. Dry, sandy soil
  3. Very low water; water sparingly
  4. Fertilize in spring/summer
  5. Keep in bright, warm spot indoors
