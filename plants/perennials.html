Perennials are the backbone of a lasting garden. These plants return year after year with the right care, offering consistent beauty and structure to your outdoor space.

Siberian Iris

  1. Full sun to part shade (6+ hours ideal)
  2. Moist, well-draining soil
  3. Water regularly, especially first year
  4. Fertilize early spring (10-10-10)
  5. Deadhead flowers; cut back in fall
  6. Divide every 3-5 years
  7. Cold-hardy in Zone 6a

Creeping Phlox

  1. Full sun preferred
  2. Sandy, well-draining soil
  3. Water during dry spells
  4. Fertilize lightly in early spring
  5. Trim after bloom to shape
  6. Divide every 2-3 years
  7. Cold-hardy in Zone 6a

Peony

  1. Full sun (6-8+ hours)
  2. Rich, well-draining soil
  3. Moderate watering; avoid soggy roots
  4. Low-nitrogen fertilizer in spring
  5. Deadhead and cut back in fall
  6. Divide every 10-15 years
  7. Cold-hardy in Zone 6a

Clematis

  1. Full sun top, shaded roots
  2. Neutral to alkaline, compost-rich soil
  3. Water deeply 1-2 times weekly
  4. Fertilize monthly if desired
  5. Prune lightly in early spring
  6. Train on trellis or fence
  7. Cold-hardy in Zone 6a

Shrub Rose

  1. Full sun (6-8 hours/day)
  2. Loamy, compost-rich soil
  3. Water weekly at base
  4. Fertilize in spring; optional re-feed
  5. Deadhead and prune early spring
  6. Disease-resistant, but good airflow helps
  7. Cold-hardy in Zone 6a
