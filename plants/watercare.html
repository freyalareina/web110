Water Care

Water is essential for growing healthy, thriving plants—but it’s more nuanced than it seems. Just like people, different plants have their own preferences. Rex begonias, impatiens, and tomatoes are heavy drinkers, while purslane, lantana, and geraniums do better when their soil dries out a bit between waterings.

Water deeply and infrequently. Most well-meaning plant lovers accidentally kill their plants by overwatering. Watch for yellow, wilted leaves, crepey brown edges, mushy stems, or mold on the soil—these are signs of root rot. It means your plant has drowned. Plants need to breathe (just like us!), and oxygen must move through their roots and out their leaves. Proper watering is only part of the equation—airflow is essential to help them thrive.
