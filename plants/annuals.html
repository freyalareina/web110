Annuals bring bright, seasonal beauty to your garden. These fast growers live for one season and reward you with non-stop color, ideal for containers, borders, and sunny beds.

Calibrachoa (Million Bells)

  1. Thrives in full sun (6+ hours)
  2. Well-draining, slightly acidic soil
  3. Consistent moisture; avoid sogginess
  4. Heavy feeder: fertilize every 1-2 weeks
  5. No deadheading needed
  6. Annual in Zone 6a
  7. Watch for aphids and powdery mildew

Gazania (Treasure Flower)

  1. Full sun; flowers close at night
  2. Sandy, well-drained soil
  3. Low water; drought-tolerant
  4. Light fertilizer monthly
  5. Deadhead to prolong blooms
  6. Annual in Zone 6a
  7. Generally pest-free

African Marigold

  1. Full sun for best blooms
  2. Tolerates poor, well-drained soil
  3. Water when dry; dislikes soggy roots
  4. Rarely needs fertilizer
  5. Deadhead to rebloom
  6. Annual in all zones
  7. Repels garden pests naturally

Dahlia

  1. Full sun; afternoon shade in peak heat
  2. Rich, composted soil
  3. Water deeply; avoid crown rot
  4. Fertilize every 2-3 weeks
  5. Pinch and deadhead for more blooms
  6. Annual in Zone 6a
  7. Watch for slugs and mildew

Fuchsia

  1. Bright shade or part sun
  2. Rich, well-draining potting mix
  3. Consistent moisture; never fully dry
  4. Feed every 1-2 weeks
  5. Deadhead and pinch to shape
  6. Annual in Zone 6a
  7. Watch for whiteflies and mites
